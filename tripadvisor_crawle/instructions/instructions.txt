## Writing Style Guidelines 🗞️

### Voice and Tone 🗣️

**Our Voice Is:**

- Developer-to-developer (expert-to-expert)
- **Personal and experience-based** - "When I built this scraper..." not "One can build a scraper..."
- Conversational but technical
- Authentic and genuine, sharing real challenges and solutions
- Honest about mistakes, failures, and what didn't work
- Community-focused and collaborative

**Write from YOUR Experience:**

✅ **Good - Personal and Specific:**

"When I built a scraper for monitoring competitor prices, I initially tried using requests but hit a wall with their JavaScript-rendered product pages. After wasting two days debugging, I switched to Crawlee with Playwright and had it working in an afternoon. Here's what I learned..."

"I ran into a frustrating issue where my Apify Actor kept timing out after 100 pages. Turns out I was storing too much in memory. Here's how I fixed it..."

❌ **Avoid - Generic and Impersonal:**

"Web scraping can be challenging when dealing with JavaScript-rendered content. One approach is to use headless browsers. This tutorial will show you how to implement such a solution."

"There are several ways to handle pagination in web scraping. Let's explore the most common methods..."
**Share Your Journey:**

- What problem were you actually trying to solve?
- What did you try first that didn't work?
- What was the "aha!" moment?
- What would you do differently next time?
- What surprised you during implementation?

### Grammar and Style Rules 🖊️

**Sentence Structure:**

- Prefer active voice: "Playwright launches a browser" not "A browser is launched by Playwright"
- Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
- Vary sentence length for rhythm
- Front-load important information

**Technical Terminology:**

- Define acronyms on first use: "Document Object Model (DOM)"
- Use industry-standard terms consistently
- Explain complex concepts briefly
- Don't over-explain basic programming concepts

**Product Names:**

- Apify (always capitalized)
- Crawlee (always capitalized)
- Actors (capitalized when referring to Apify Actors)
- Playwright, Puppeteer, Selenium (standard capitalizations)
### Formatting Conventions ℹ️

**Text Formatting:**

- **Bold** for emphasis on key terms, important concepts (use sparingly)
- Code font for inline code, commands, filenames, package names
- *Italics* rarely used (only for emphasis when bold is too strong)

**Headers:**

- H1: Article title only (handled by CMS)
- H2: Major sections (sentence case)
- H3: Subsections (sentence case)
- Never use title case: "Setting up your environment" not "Setting Up Your Environment"

**Lists:**

- Bullet points for unordered items, features, options
- Numbered lists for sequential steps, procedures
- Keep list items parallel in structure
- Each item should be substantial (avoid 1-2 word items)
## Do's and Don'ts ✌️

### Content Do's ✅

- **Do** write about YOUR actual experience with Apify/Crawlee
- **Do** share YOUR specific project, challenges, and solutions
- **Do** include concrete details from YOUR implementation (timelines, costs, data volumes)
- **Do** describe what YOU tried that didn't work first
- **Do** use first-person voice ("I built", "I learned", "I discovered")
- **Do** provide complete, working code from YOUR actual implementation
- **Do** include a GitHub repository with your full project code if applicable
- **Do** show real debugging processes and errors YOU encountered
- **Do** write developer-to-developer in YOUR authentic voice
- **Do** use multiple screenshots from YOUR actual implementation
- **Do** test all code before submitting
- **Do** share what YOU would do differently next time
- **Do** link to relevant Apify/Crawlee resources naturally
- **Do** explain YOUR decision-making process
- **Do** be honest about YOUR mistakes and learnings
- **Do** include specific Apify Actors or Crawlee features YOU used
### Content Don'ts ❌

- **Don't** write generic tutorials without personal experience
- **Don't** use AI to generate content you haven't personally validated
- **Don't** include AI writing clichés ("let's dive in", "in today's landscape")
- **Don't** write about tools you haven't actually used
- **Don't** submit articles without clear Apify/Crawlee connection
- **Don't** write in third-person or passive voice ("one can build")
- **Don't** submit previously published content
- **Don't** use copyrighted images without permission
- **Don't** write for complete beginners with no coding experience
- **Don't** submit generic code snippets without YOUR implementation context
- **Don't** use generic anchor text like "click here"
- **Don't** keyword stuff for SEO
- **Don't** make claims without backing them up with YOUR experience
- **Don't** ignore accessibility (alt text, clear language)
- **Don't** use overly salesy or promotional language
- **Don't** submit incomplete or untested code
- **Don't** skip error handling in YOUR examples
- **Don't** rehash official documentation without adding YOUR insights
- **Don't** write about "best practices" without showing YOUR implementation
